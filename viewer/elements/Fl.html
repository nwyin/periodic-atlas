No commercial production.

Flerovium has no commercial supply chain and no practical traded form. The element exists only in nuclear-physics experiments that synthesize a few atoms at a time and identify them through correlated alpha-decay chains. That keeps the commercial fields empty even though the element is formally named and recognized. The primary commercial reality is therefore absence: no mining, no refining, no reserves, no price series, and no end-use market. Its significance is scientific rather than industrial, because synthesis and decay measurements help test shell effects and the "island of stability" hypothesis for superheavy nuclei.
