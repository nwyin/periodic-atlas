Pm promethium
Atomic number 61 • lanthanide • Tier 2
Commercial
Wikipedia↗
Promethium is commercially real in 2025, but only as a tiny isotope business centered on Pm-147. Customers do not buy promethium metal by the tonne; they buy curie quantities of a radioactive chloride or nitrate salt from the DOE isotope catalog. The isotope's demand is tied to its behavior as a low-energy beta emitter, which is why official DOE and ORNL sources consistently frame the market around thickness gauges, compact nuclear batteries and other long-lived low-power devices, luminous products, and a handful of specialized imaging uses. The supply chain is reactor and hot-cell infrastructure, not mining. DOE's initial commercial batches came from recovery of Pm-147 from a plutonium-program waste stream after international disruptions hit the prior Russia-centered supply route. In parallel, ORNL has developed a direct HFIR route using enriched Nd-146 targets that decay through Nd-147 to Pm-147 before chemical purification. By 2025 DOE described itself as the only global producer of Pm-147, underscoring how concentrated and policy-shaped this market is.

Isotope Markets (1)

Pm-147

reactor_generated
Half-life: 2.62 years
Precursor: DOE said in 2022 that its initial commercial Pm-147 supply was being extracted from a plutonium waste stream associated with the Pu-238 program, and that it would also be produced through direct irradiation of enriched Nd-146 in ORNL's High Flux Isotope Reactor. The ORNL reactor-production paper describes the Nd-146(n,gamma)Nd-147 to Pm-147 route and the post-irradiation extraction-chromatography purification step.
Delivery form: DOE/NIDC sells Pm-147 as chloride or nitrate salt, with radionuclide purity of 99.99%, routinely available status, and curies as the unit of sale. The sheet lists >=483 Ci/g for 147PmCl3 and >=834.3 Ci/g for elemental 147Pm.
