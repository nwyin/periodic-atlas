No commercial production.

Bohrium is a research-only transactinide with no mine supply, refining base, reserves, or price series. Its commercial reality is the absence of commerce: laboratories make individual atoms in accelerator campaigns to probe nuclear stability, decay chains, and the chemistry of the superheavy region. [Sources: gsi_new_elements, iupac_transfermium_1997] The accepted discovery route is GSI Darmstadt's 1981 production of element 107 via chromium-on-bismuth bombardment. IUPAC formalized the name bohrium and symbol Bh in 1997, and IUPAC's present periodic table identifies mass number 270 as the longest-lived confirmed bohrium nuclide. NNDC ENSDF shows a corresponding "270BH A DECAY (1.0 M)" dataset, supporting the one-minute order-of-magnitude lifetime used in this record. [Sources: gsi_new_elements, iupac_transfermium_1997, iupac_periodic_table_2022, nndc_270bh_decay_2019]
