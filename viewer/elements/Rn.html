No commercial production.

Radon is a naturally arising radioactive noble gas, not a commercial gas business. The atlas treatment is research-only because the element has no stable isotope, the IUPAC table anchors it to mass number 222 rather than a standard atomic weight, and the longest-lived nuclide lasts only 3.8222 days. That lifetime is long enough for environmental measurement and laboratory use but far too short for a durable mined-and-refined commodity chain. The historical discovery path also differs from mined elements. IUPAC's history of radioactivity credits Rutherford with thorium emanation in 1899 and Ernst Dorn with radium emanation in 1900; the radium-derived gas was the longer-lived isotope later identified as radon-222. Commercial reality therefore centers on on-site generation from radioactive parents and on hazard monitoring, not on reserves, prices, or traded end-use markets.
