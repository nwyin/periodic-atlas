Commercial
Wikipedia↗
Cesium is commercially real, but it is not a bulk metal market. The modern trade is a thin specialty-chemicals business built on pollucite feedstock and sold mainly as carbonate, hydroxide, formate, iodide, chloride, nitrate, and other derivative salts. USGS still characterizes the United States as 100% import reliant, notes that elemental cesium itself is not traded in commercial quantities, and withholds conventional production, consumption, and trade series because the market is so small and opaque. What can be seen publicly is a supply chain with very few moving parts. Canada remains the anchor because Tanco is still one of the only named mined ore and stockpile processing centers in current USGS reporting, while Zimbabwe tailings recovery for shipment to China has re-emerged as a second feed. The most recent quantified flow USGS publishes is not a mine tonnage but the replacement demand implied by cesium formate drilling brines already in circulation. That is typical of cesium: commercial significance is driven by niche functionality in catalysts, dense clear brines, X-ray panels, scintillators, and timing or isotope applications, not by large tonnage.
