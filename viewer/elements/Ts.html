No commercial production.

Tennessine is a research-only superheavy halogen with no extractive, refining, or end-use market. It is made by fusing calcium-48 ions with a berkelium-249 target at extremely low event rates, then identifying the new nuclide through correlated alpha-decay chains. The isotope with the longest confirmed half-life in the primary nuclear-data sources is Ts-294 at about 51 milliseconds, which is still far too short for any bulk handling or chemistry-based commercialization. Commercial reality is therefore limited to state-funded accelerator campaigns, target-material production, and detector development supporting superheavy-element physics.
