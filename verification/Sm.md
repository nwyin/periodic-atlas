## Notes

**Samarium oxide price discrepancy (root cause):** The USGS MCS 2025 Rare Earths Salient Statistics price table lists prices for seven oxides only: Ce (99.5%), Dy (99.5%), Eu (99.99%), La (99.5%), Mischmetal, Nd (99.5%), and Tb (99.99%). Samarium oxide (Sm₂O₃, 99.9% minimum) does NOT appear in this table. All five price entries in Sm.yaml (2020–2024) cite `usgs_mcs_2025_rare_earths` as their source and claim to reference "USGS MCS 2025 salient statistics" — but Sm₂O₃ is absent from that table. The prices themselves ($2–$4/kg range) may be accurate per Argus Media Group's Argus Non-Ferrous Markets data (footnote 6 source for the seven listed oxides), but they cannot be directly verified from the USGS MCS 2025 Rare Earths chapter. A direct Argus Media source would be needed for these claims.

**April 2025 China export controls (root cause):** China announced specific export controls naming samarium and six other REEs (Gd, Tb, Dy, Lu, Sc, Y) in April 2025. USGS MCS 2025 was published January 2025 and cannot document this event. The geopolitical_events[0] entry cites `usgs_mcs_2025_rare_earths` but the event postdates that publication by three months. This is a source attribution error; a different source (Chinese Ministry of Commerce announcement, REE trade news) is required.

**ZZ notes arithmetic error (informational, not a discrepancy):** The ZZ rest-of-world production notes in Sm.yaml state "~9,050 t total REO" but the individual components sum to 8,950 t (India 2,900 + Russia 2,500 + Madagascar 2,000 + Other 1,100 + Vietnam 300 + Malaysia 130 + Brazil 20 = 8,950). This is the same 100 t arithmetic error present in Pr.yaml ZZ notes (documented in prior hive notes). The share_pct (2.3%) and Sm quantity (251 t) are computed correctly against the true 8,950 t total. The error is in the notes text only, not in the claimed values.

**Sm-specific basket share (2.8%):** Unlike Ce (~33%), La (~29%), Nd (~18%), or Pr (~4.5%), USGS does not report or use a Sm basket share. All Sm-specific production and reserve quantities are derived from this industry consensus figure. The 2.8% global weighted average reflects: Mountain Pass (~0.5%), Bayan Obo (~2.5%), Mt. Weld (~2.3%), and southern China/SE Asia ion adsorption clays (~3–5%). All Sm quantities carry low confidence and inferred status.

**Refining share denominator caveat:** Reserve share percentages use 90,000,000 t as the denominator (the stated lower bound). Since the USGS reports ">90,000,000 t," actual world reserves may be higher, meaning all reported share_pct values are slight overestimates. This is consistent with the treatment in the Pr.md verification.

**Europium oxide price in USGS table (cross-check):** Eu₂O₃ (99.99% min) IS listed in the USGS MCS 2025 salient statistics table with prices: 2020=$31, 2021=$31, 2022=$30, 2023=$27, 2024e=$27/kg. This confirms the table's coverage (confirmed by Eu verification worker). Sm₂O₃ is simply not included in the same table.
