## Notes

**Source access**: USGS MCS 2025 silicon PDF (https://pubs.usgs.gov/periodicals/mcs2025/mcs2025-silicon.pdf) was downloaded (725 KB) and extracted via pdftotext. Full two-page text extracted successfully including the World Production table, Salient Statistics table, and Events/Trends narrative. EU CRM Act EUR-Lex URL returned empty content on all fetch attempts; both EU criticality claims are marked `source_unreachable`.

**Unit conversion**: USGS reports all figures in "thousand metric tons, silicon content." YAML stores in tonnes (× 1000). Conversion is exact: e.g., USGS "5,100" kt → YAML 5,100,000 tonnes.

**US production withheld**: USGS withholds US production data for both ferrosilicon and silicon metal to avoid disclosing company proprietary data. All world totals in YAML correctly exclude the US. USGS confirms five facilities operated in 2024 (all east of the Mississippi), plus one idled silicon metal facility.

**Share percentages**: No country share percentages are published in the USGS source; all are derived arithmetically by dividing each country's kt by the rounded world total. Share percentages match to 1 decimal place as reported in the YAML.

**FeSi world total sum check**: Named countries sum = CN(3500)+RU(470)+BR(200)+NO(180)+MY(130)+KZ(130)+BT(80)+IS(70)+IN(60)+ZA(40)+ES(40)+PL(30)+FR(20)+CA(20)+ZZ(130) = 5,100 kt. Matches USGS world total exactly. ✓

**Si metal world total sum check**: Named countries sum = CN(3900)+BR(190)+NO(120)+FR(90)+DE(60)+RU(50)+AU(40)+CA(30)+IS(20)+ZA(10)+KZ(7)+ES(5)+ZZ(78) = 4,600 kt. Matches USGS world total exactly. ✓

**Price source footnotes**: FeSi 75% prices (2021–2024) and silicon metal prices (all years) are sourced from S&P Global Platts Metals Week mean import prices, monthly averages. The FeSi 50% grade prices (CRU Group) were discontinued April 2022; YAML correctly uses the 75% grade series throughout. The 2024 figures are Jan–Sep 2024 averages (estimated).

**End-use share percentages**: USGS MCS 2025 does not publish global silicon end-use breakdowns with quantitative percentages. The five share_pct values in the YAML (35%, 25%, 20%, 15%, 5%) are analyst estimates derived from USGS qualitative descriptions and general industry context, acknowledged in the YAML notes with confidence: medium. All are marked `inferred`.

**Feedstock concentration 97%**: USGS describes quartzite as the silicon feedstock but does not state a purity percentage. The 97% SiO2 figure in the YAML is industry-standard knowledge not attributable to the cited USGS source; marked `inferred`.

**Geopolitical event months**: USGS MCS 2025 explicitly states "April 2024" for the Georgia solar module event (verified). For three other events (Washington polysilicon restart, Montana facility pivot, EU/Canada designation), USGS says "In 2024" without specifying a month. YAML assigns January 2024 (date: 2024-01); months are unverified from the cited source.

**EU CRMA criticality**: EUR-Lex returned empty content on multiple fetch attempts (CELEX 32024R1252). However, USGS MCS 2025 text independently states: "silicon metal was included as a strategic raw material in the European Union's Critical Raw Materials Act owing to its importance in the manufacture of semiconductor chips, the electronics market, and solar power generation." This corroborates the strategic claim (eu_strategic_list_as_of_2024 = true). Whether silicon metal also appears in Annex II (Critical, non-strategic) cannot be confirmed from available sources; the claim eu_crm_list_as_of_2024 = true is plausible (Strategic materials are a subset of Critical) but unverified from a primary EU source.

**"almost 80%" combined share**: USGS states "China accounted almost 80% of total global estimated production of silicon materials in 2024." The combined figure is (3500+3900)/(5100+4600) = 7400/9700 = 76.3%, consistent with USGS's "almost 80%." This appears only in the YAML narrative, not as a YAML data field claim.

**No reserves numeric data**: USGS MCS 2025 publishes no numeric reserves or resources figures for silicon, consistent with the YAML reserves block containing only a qualitative notes field.
