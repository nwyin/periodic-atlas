## Notes

**Source attribution note for geopolitical_events[2024]:** The 2024 geopolitical event uses `source_id: usgs_mcs_2025_yttrium`, but several figures cited in the event's impact text (world REO production 390,000 t, country breakdowns, country-level production changes) come from the Rare Earths chapter, not the Yttrium chapter. The Yttrium chapter contributes the IAC quota (19,150 t) and China Y exports (3,100 t) to this event. The attribution is partial but not incorrect — both chapters are cited as sources elsewhere in Y.yaml.

**Source attribution note for mining_by_country[MM]:** The Burma-specific REO tonnage (31,000 t in 2024e, 43,000 t in 2023) appears in the Rare Earths chapter. The YAML correctly cites the Rare Earths chapter in its notes text ("per Rare Earths chapter") but assigns `source_id: usgs_mcs_2025_yttrium` to the MM entry. The qualitative statement that Burma produces yttrium from clay deposits IS in the Yttrium chapter; the specific tonnages are from the Rare Earths chapter.

**Y₂O₃ production directly reported (key differentiator):** Unlike all other HREE elements in atlas (which require basket-share derivation), yttrium has a dedicated USGS chapter that directly reports world Y₂O₃ production as 15,000–20,000 t (2024e). This makes the mine.value estimate (17,500 t midpoint) substantially more reliable than other HREE production figures, justifying confidence: medium rather than low.

**All 10 price data points verified:** Yttrium is exceptional among HREEs in having directly reported price series in USGS MCS 2025 (both Y₂O₃ and Y metal, FOB China per Argus Rare Earths, for all five years 2020–2024e). All 10 price values match the Salient Statistics table exactly.

**Reserves explicitly unquantified by USGS:** The Yttrium chapter states "Global reserves of Y₂O₃ were not quantified." All per-country Y₂O₃ reserve figures in the YAML are therefore derived (inferred) by multiplying country-level REO reserves (from Rare Earths chapter) by the implied ~4.5% Y₂O₃ basket share. These carry low confidence as stated.

**EU CRMA source not accessible:** The EU Regulation 2024/1252 source (eu_crma_2024) returned no accessible content from the EUR-Lex URL; both EU criticality claims are marked source_unreachable. Unlike Yb.yaml and Dy.yaml (which incorrectly cited usgs_mcs_2025_rare_earths for EU CRM status), Y.yaml correctly attributes these fields to eu_crma_2024 — the attributions are correct but the document is inaccessible.

**US import data for 2024e inconsistency:** The Salient Statistics table shows 2024e imports for consumption = 470 t and apparent consumption = 500 t (vs. 250 t and 200 t in 2023). These figures are not claimed in Y.yaml but confirm ongoing US yttrium import dependence.
