**Zero discrepancies**: All 130 directly verifiable numeric claims matched their USGS source exactly. All production quantities, price series values, reserve raw quantities, import share percentages, geopolitical figures, and feedstock origin percentages agree with the USGS chapter text.

**All share_pcts are inferred**: USGS does not publish country percentage shares for ilmenite, rutile, sponge, TiO2 capacity, or reserves in these chapters. Every share_pct in the YAML is derived by dividing country quantity by world total. All calculations are arithmetically correct.

**US TiO2 share_pct basis note**: The production[titanium_dioxide].refining_by_country[US].share_pct = 13.88 corresponds to US TiO2 pigment CAPACITY (1,360,000 t/yr) / world capacity (9,800,000 t/yr) = 13.878%, not to the US production quantity (850,000 t) / world capacity = 8.67%. The YAML records actual US production (850,000 t) as the quantity but the capacity-based share (13.88%), creating an internal inconsistency. Both individual values (1,360,000 capacity and 850,000 production) are correct as sourced from USGS; the inconsistency is a representational choice within the YAML, not a factual error. The notes section of production[titanium_dioxide] acknowledges this.

**Reserve share_pct denominator**: The YAML note states "shares are computed against the stated world total of >560,000 kt" but the arithmetic does not support this: AU (215,000 / 560,000 = 38.39% ≠ 36.91%). The shares appear to be computed against an implied denominator of approximately 582,800 kt, which would represent the sum of all listed country reserves plus an estimated "Other countries" contribution beyond the USGS minimum (>54,000 + >540 kt). Since the exact Other value is not disclosed and ">" minimums cannot uniquely determine a denominator, all reserve share_pcts are inferred. The raw country reserve quantities are fully verified.

**Rutile sum check**: 200+100+60+40+20+10+12+8 = 450 kt = exactly 100% of stated world total. No rounding gap.

**Ilmenite sum check**: 3300+1900+1300+400+360+350+300+240+210+120+100+330 = 8,910 kt vs stated world total 8,900 kt (rounded). Difference of 10 kt (0.11%) is a rounding artifact.

**Sponge sum check**: 220,000+55,000+20,000+15,000+14,000+300 = 324,300 t vs stated world total 320,000 t (rounded, excludes US). Difference of 4,300 t (1.3%) is within rounding tolerance at this scale.

**TiO2 capacity sum check**: Sum of all country capacities = 5,500,000+1,360,000+350,000+339,000+315,000+322,000+260,000+200,000+122,000+108,000+91,000+55,000+820,000+500 = 9,842,500 t vs stated 9,800,000. Difference of 42,500 (0.43%) is within rounding tolerance.

**Feedstock concentration percentages**: ilmenite 45% is the conventional lower end of the FeTiO3 range (45–65% TiO2) and is widely cited but not stated as a USGS point value in this chapter. Slag 87% is the midpoint of the USGS-defined price range "80%–95% TiO2". Both are marked inferred.

**End-use percentages**: USGS MCS 2025 titanium chapter names TiO2 uses only qualitatively in descending order (paints, plastics, paper) with no percentage breakdown. All six end-use share_pcts are editorial estimates by the YAML author and are correctly flagged as confidence: medium or low in the source YAML.

**Narrative error (not a source_id claim)**: The narrative section states "Apparent consumption of ~340,000 t/yr" in the context of the US sponge metal situation. However, the USGS table shows US sponge apparent consumption = 40,000 t in 2024 (excl. domestic production). The ~340,000 t figure is likely a confusion with world sponge production (~320,000 t). Since the narrative section carries no source_id, this is outside the formal claims table scope but should be noted for a follow-up correction pass.

**Import sources (mineral concentrates, 2020–23 annual average)**: USGS states "South Africa, 32%; Madagascar, 16%; Canada, 13%; Australia, 11%; and other, 28%." These differ from the September 2024 figures cited in the YAML (ilmenite: MZ 38%, MG 36%, SN 22%; rutile: ZA 53%, AU 29%, KE 8%, UA 8%), which reflect a single-month snapshot rather than the 4-year average. The YAML correctly uses the more recent Sep 2024 data in the feedstock_origins and geopolitical sections.
