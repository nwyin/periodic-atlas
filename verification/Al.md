## Notes

**Source access**: Both PDFs were fetched and rendered fully. USGS MCS 2025 Aluminum chapter (pp. 32–33) and Bauxite and Alumina chapter (pp. 42–43) are accessible. All claims trace to one of these two primary sources.

**Three-stream structure**: Al.yaml uses three `production` entries (stream: bauxite, alumina, aluminum_metal). Bauxite quantities are in million_tonnes_per_year (USGS reports in thousand metric dry tons; YAML divides by 1,000). Reserves use integer tonnes (USGS reports in thousand metric dry tons; YAML multiplies by 1,000). The conversion is applied consistently throughout.

**Australia reserves superscript**: The USGS table shows "73,500,000" for Australia in the reserves column; the leading "7" is footnote superscript 7 (JORC note), not a digit. Actual value is 3,500,000 thousand metric dry tons = 3.5 billion tonnes. YAML correctly records 3500000000. ✓

**DOE grant total ($664M)**: USGS lists four individual grants ($500M + $75M + $67.3M + $22.3M = $664.6M). The YAML rounds to $664M. This is an arithmetic approximation, not a stated USGS figure, but it correctly summarises the four grants. No discrepancy.

**Alumina 2024 price ($570 vs $560)**: The salient statistics table gives the full-year 2024e figure as $570/mt; the events narrative gives the first-8-month figure as $560/mt. The YAML correctly captures both: value=570 (annual estimate) with notes citing $560 for the 8-month period. No discrepancy.

**Share_pcts**: All 46 share_pct fields across the three production streams and the reserves are derived (not stated by USGS). All arithmetic checks pass: bauxite country sum = 450,000 kt (sum of listed countries = 444,300 + ZZ 5,700 = 450,000 ✓); alumina sum = 142,000 kt ✓; aluminum sum = 72,000 kt ✓; reserves sum = 29,000,000 kt ✓.

**World Resources (aluminum chapter)**: The aluminum chapter also states "Global resources of bauxite are estimated to be between 55 billion and 75 billion tons" — identical language to the bauxite chapter. Both chapters corroborate the YAML resources claim.

**Criticality**: No source_id fields appear in the criticality block of Al.yaml. The three boolean flags are marked `inferred` based on well-established public lists; a dedicated source ID pointing to the USGS critical minerals program or EU Regulation 2024/1252 would be needed for `verified` status.
