## Notes

**Critical source attribution issue:** The USGS MCS 2025 publishes a dedicated *Scandium* chapter (`mcs2025-scandium.pdf`) which is the authoritative source for all Sc-specific numeric data. The Sc.yaml file cites only `usgs_mcs_2025_rare_earths` (the Rare Earths chapter, `mcs2025-rare-earths.pdf`) for all Sc-specific claims. The Rare Earths chapter explicitly states in footnote 1: "Data include lanthanides and yttrium but exclude most scandium. See also the Scandium and Yttrium chapters." Nearly all Sc-specific numeric claims therefore cannot be sourced to the cited document. A separate source entry for `usgs_mcs_2025_scandium` should be added in a future correction pass.

**Major discrepancy — world Sc₂O₃ production (18 t vs 40 t):** Sc.yaml states ~18 t (range 15–20 t) world production in 2024e. The USGS Scandium chapter Events, Trends, and Issues section states: "According to industry estimates, global production totaled 40 tons." Global capacity was 80 t/yr and consumption 30–40 t/yr. The 18 t figure is approximately half the USGS-cited industry estimate. This discrepancy flows through all derived country-level production quantities (which should also be ~2× higher).

**Prices discrepancy:** `prices: []` and the form_notes claim that USGS does not include Sc in its salient statistics price table are accurately scoped to the *Rare Earths* chapter's price table (which covers only Ce, Dy, Eu, La, Nd, mischmetal, and Tb). However, the USGS Scandium chapter has an explicit Salient Statistics table with Sc compound and metal prices: Sc₂O₃ (99.99%, 5-kg) = $1.20/g (2024e), declining from $3.80/g (2020). Sc metal (ingot, 5-g) = $153/g; Sc-Al alloy (1,000-kg) = $32/kg (2024e). These prices are not reflected in Sc.yaml.

**Australia reserves discrepancy:** Sc.yaml claims AU = 10,000 t Sc₂O₃ (~10% of 100,000 t world total). The USGS Scandium chapter states Australian EDR at ~37,000 t Sc₂O₃ as of December 2023, with JORC-compliant reserves of 12,000 t (footnote 7). Even the JORC figure (12,000 t) exceeds Sc.yaml's 10,000 t estimate.

**Country production data unavailability:** The USGS Scandium chapter explicitly states: "Foreign mine production data for 2023 and 2024 were not available." All country-level Sc₂O₃ production shares and quantities in Sc.yaml (CN 12 t, PH 3 t, RU 1 t, KZ 1 t, ZZ 1 t) are therefore inferred from external industry estimates, not USGS data. The USGS Scandium chapter does qualitatively confirm China as "the leading producer" and lists Philippines (HPAL nickel laterite, Taganito plant), Kazakhstan (uranium), Russia (apatite and uranium), and Ukraine (uranium) as historical producers.

**Scandium chapter confirms principal end uses:** The Scandium chapter states "The principal uses for scandium in 2024 were in aluminum-scandium alloys and solid oxide fuel cells (SOFCs). Other uses for scandium included ceramics, electronics, lasers, lighting, and radioactive isotopes." This is qualitatively consistent with the end-use claims in Sc.yaml but no percentage breakdown is provided by USGS; all share percentages (65/20/10/5) are industry estimates.

**China April 2025 export controls (source_unreachable):** The china_mofcom_2025_ree_controls source has url=null, consistent with all other HREE element verifications in this project. The event is well-attested in contemporaneous press reporting and is consistent with prior worker notes.

**EU CRMA source (source_unreachable):** The EU CRMA 2024 source (EUR-Lex) returned empty content during this verification pass, consistent with prior workers' experience (Ho.md, Tm.md). Sc.yaml correctly uses `eu_crma_2024` (not `usgs_mcs_2025_rare_earths`) for EU criticality claims, avoiding the source misattribution error flagged in Yb.yaml.

**US 2024 domestic production correctly stated as zero:** Sc chapter confirms: "Domestically, scandium was neither mined nor recovered from process streams or mine tailings in 2024." and "Net import reliance: 100" — consistent with Sc.yaml criticality claims.

**Scandium chapter import sources differ from Rare Earths chapter:** For REE compounds and metals overall, US imports (2020–23): China 70%, Malaysia 13%, Japan 6%, Estonia 5%. For Sc specifically (Sc chapter): "imported material was mostly from Japan, China, and Philippines" — Philippines and Japan are more prominent in Sc-specific trade than in the combined REE import mix.
